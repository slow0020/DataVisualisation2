International Students in Australia

International students form a vital part of Australia’s education landscape,
contributing significantly to the economy and cultural diversity. This visualization
explores the evolving trends of student visa holders across various categories over the
years, providing insights into how Australia attracts students for higher education,
vocational training, and other specialized programs. This analysis offers a
comprehensive overview of the shifts in international student enrollment.
Aiming to highlight the impact of key events, such as
global economic changes and the COVID-19 pandemic, on student mobility.

This interactive tool helps policymakers, educational institutions, and stakeholders
understand the distribution patterns, regional preferences, and long-term trends in Australia’s
international student population, empowering them to make informed decisions and
foster sustainable growth in the education sector.

This visualization provides an overview of the number of international students
distributed across different states and territories in Australia from 2005 to 2024.
It reveals key geographic patterns and visa-type distributions for international students.

Total Number of International Students in Each State (2024)

This visualization provides an overview of the number of international students
distributed across different States and Territories in Australia in 2024.
New South Wales has the largest number of international students in Australia
with Victoria coming in second with 183,220 students on a Temporary Student Visa.

Total Number of International Students by State (2005-2024)

The number of students in Australia has generally increased over time, but there was a sharp decline in 2020-2021 due to the COVID pandemic. New South Wales and Victoria consistently have the highest student numbers, while smaller states like Tasmania and Northern Territory have much fewer students.
